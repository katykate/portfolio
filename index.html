Frequently Asked Questions

Thank you for visiting my page

How do you define a webpage?

To us a webpage is a page that generally will viewable in your monitor. However, that does not necessarily mean that it cannot be longer, and include scrollbars to the right hand side. Unless you have a television size screen, it would be pretty impossible to create a page that never needed scrolling (unless of course you wanted just a logo). So a webpage to us is the content that is aimed for that page. An example is this page. It will have scrollbars undoubtedly, but why spread it over "several" pages just so I don't need to use my mouse-wheel!

How long does it takes to design a website?

The timescale will depend on many factors, such as how much work are we currently covering, do we need to keep reviewing and altering page designs, and other factors that may crop up. Generally speaking, if the client has a clear idea of what they want, and we receive the necessary additional images and information from them, that they would like to appear on their site, we can probably turn it around in two to four weeks. Sometimes however, a site may be more complex, and it might take longer, but generally speaking the basics of a website can be done within 35 days.

Do I need to meet you to discuss anything?

In the majority of cases, there is no need for you to do this. Most of the work (especially if we are creating sites for people who are not local to us) is undertaken by means of email communications, perhaps phone calls, or even using Skype or MSN. Yes we have been visited by clients, or visited them, and held discussions face-to-face, but this is not a necessity, unless of course you want to pay for our travelling costs in which case we would gratefully accept.

Once my page is designed, what happens next?

Once your website is up and running I can offer you a full support package designed to help maintain and update as required certain elements of your website. Basically we can act as "agents" for your site, taking care of further updates, additional modifications if required, and report back to you on page rankings, visitors and the like. If we are taking care of your hosting for you, it makes the support even easier.

Do I have to keep my site with you forever?

Once I have designed, and uploaded your website to server, or a hosting company, one of your choosing, I of course would love you to stay with me. Having continuity with one person, can be really beneficial (providing of course you are happy with my services), and if there are changes that need to be made at a later date, it's easier if you and me are familiar with what is going on. However, you may wish to decide to move away from me, in which case this is acceptable. You will own the completed site though, I will still retain the copyright on my design, or any images supplied by me, or any software incorporated on the site.

Will you place ads on my site?

I will NOT place ads on your site unless you specifically request us to do so (this may have been something that you asked me to do as part of your web design stipulation, or you specifically asked me to do this for you).

In which format would I have to supply images to you for my site?

It is preferable to have images supplied in JPEG format, GIF or BMP. We find that if people can email us their images (image sizes are better off bigger - they can always be reduced in size rather than increased). Most digital cameras will produce adequate resolution images (the higher the resolution the better the image quality) and these images can be sent to us by electronic methods, or by post on a CD if there are lots of images. If you get stuck we can always help you with images, and we have a graphic design team available to do so.
